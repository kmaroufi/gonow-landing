The Data Behind Your Bathroom Anxiety

52% missed a goal. 95% hold it in. The study that proved this was never just paranoia.

Research · June 2026 · 2 min read

In 2023, a team of statisticians analyzed 106 MLS matches per team and asked how many goals a fan would miss by taking a bathroom break at a normal moment. The answer was bleak.

52% of football fans have already missed their team scoring while they were in the bathroom. Of the rest, 95% say they now hold it in during matches to avoid the same thing. That number tells you this isn't paranoia. It's something people have learned from experience.

The worst affected: Orlando City fans risk missing 4.6 goals per season if they take breaks at random moments. Atlanta United and Inter Miami come in at 4.2. The safest are New England Revolution supporters at 1.5 missed goals per season.

When goals actually happen

Victorian Plumbing studied Premier League goal data and found that 8% of goals come in the first 10 minutes, the quietest statistical window in a match. If you're going to guess, early is better.

But 70% of match action, including late pressure, comeback goals, and injury-time drama, comes after the 70th minute. The longer a match goes without a goal, the more compressed the remaining action tends to get.

The math

A typical bathroom break takes about 2 minutes 23 seconds. Most people need to urinate roughly every two hours. A World Cup match runs 90 minutes of normal time plus stoppage, and you still need to walk there, join the queue, and wait.

You will need to go at some point. The question is picking the right moment.

Why historical data doesn't solve it

Past stats tell you what teams have done across a season. They don't read what this specific match is doing right now. The 80th minute might be statistically quiet, but tonight's game doesn't care about averages.
