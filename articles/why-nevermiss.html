You Stepped Out. Argentina Scored.

Why 52% of fans have been there, why nobody solved it until now, and what NeverMiss actually reads.

Origin Story · June 2026 · 2 min read

You know the feeling. You weigh it for three minutes: bladder at maybe a 6, match at 0-0, forty-first minute, possession bouncing around midfield. Seems safe. You go. You're gone ninety seconds. The stadium erupts.

You hear it before you see it.

This isn't bad luck. A study of 106 MLS matches per team found that 52% of football fans have missed their team scoring while they were in the bathroom, and 95% now hold it in because they've been burned before.

The problem nobody solved

RunPee exists for cinema. It identifies the slow scenes in a film: the setup, the filler, the moments a director built in to let audiences breathe. It works because cinema is scripted, with objectively quiet stretches baked in.

The Detroit Pistons put real-time bathroom queue lengths in their app. Smart. You know which line is shortest before you commit. But that's a where problem, not a when problem. You still don't know if the game is about to blow open.

Live football has no director. It has ninety minutes of rolling tension with no scripted breathing room, and no existing app touches that.

What NeverMiss reads

Five live signals run simultaneously: possession clustering near the box, shot cadence versus match average, defensive pressure in dangerous zones, consecutive attacking sequences without a counter, dead-ball density in set-piece range.

When all five drop together, that's your window. Not a historical average. Not which minute is usually quiet. What's happening in this specific match, right now.

Why it matters now

FIFA World Cup 2026 is live. 104 matches, 48 teams, the biggest tournament in history. Your bladder will not cooperate through all of it.

NeverMiss is the app a fan built because nobody else did. Free, no install, works at the stadium, the pub, or the couch. It tells you when it's safe to step away.
